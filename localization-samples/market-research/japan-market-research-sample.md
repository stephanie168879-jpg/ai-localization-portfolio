Japan Market Research Sample

Project Type

Japan-side market research / competitor research / local business information gathering

Scenario

This sample demonstrates how I structure a basic Japan market research report for an international client who wants to understand the local market before entering Japan or making a business decision.

The example is based on a fictional client who is considering launching a productivity app for small business owners in Japan.

Research Objective

To understand the Japanese market environment for a productivity app targeting small businesses, freelancers, and small teams.

Key Research Questions

1. Who are the potential users in Japan?
2. What problems do they currently face?
3. What types of competing tools are already available?
4. What localization points should be considered?
5. What risks or challenges should the client be aware of?

Target Users

Potential users may include:

* Small business owners
* Freelancers
* Solo entrepreneurs
* Small consulting teams
* Language teachers or coaches
* Local service providers
* Administrative staff in small companies

User Pain Points

Japanese small business users may need support with:

* Task organization
* Document management
* Customer communication
* Schedule coordination
* Invoice and payment tracking
* Reducing repetitive manual work
* Improving communication between team members

Competitor Research Structure

When reviewing competitors, I would check:

Item	What to Check
Product name	Main service or app name
Target users	Who the product is designed for
Pricing	Free plan, monthly plan, enterprise plan
Main features	Task management, automation, templates, AI tools
Japanese localization	Natural Japanese, help pages, customer support
Customer reviews	Common complaints and positive feedback
Differentiation	What makes the product stand out

Localization Considerations

For the Japanese market, the product should consider:

* Polite and clear Japanese wording
* Simple onboarding flow
* Trust-building language
* Examples relevant to Japanese business users
* Mobile-friendly user experience
* Clear pricing and cancellation policy
* FAQ and customer support in Japanese

Sample Findings

Based on the research structure above, the product should not be positioned only as an “AI tool.”
For many Japanese small business users, it may be more effective to position it as a practical tool that helps reduce daily workload, organize information, and improve communication.

Instead of saying:

This AI app improves your productivity.

A more localized message could be:

日々の業務をよりスムーズに進めるために、タスク管理や情報整理をサポートします。

Recommended Next Steps

1. Identify 5 to 10 direct competitors in Japan.
2. Review their pricing, features, and user reviews.
3. Analyze Japanese landing pages and support pages.
4. Prepare localized messaging for Japanese users.
5. Test Japanese copy with target users before launch.
